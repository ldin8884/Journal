Process Journal

Introduction

My name is Aiden Dinh. The purpose of this Process Journal is to record the progression of our team's project - Interactive Prototype. Weekly workload will be documented below to show the development of our project throughout time.

WEEK 1

This is the first week of the semester. There was nothing much to do besides reading the design brief. Thus, I did nothing to contribute for the final prototype.

WEEK 2

After the discussion within our team, we decided to pick up the autonomous food delivery drone topic. I've started the first assignment with a form of semi-structure questionnaire and interview.

Previous Next

WEEK 3

Until this point, my research report has been done half-way. I have finished the background research and started transcipting the online questionnaire which was published on my social media page last week. Additionally, I created 2 personas based on obtained information during the research process.

Previous Next

WEEK 4

During this week, I reviewed my research report paper and fixed any typo and grammar issues within it. Two storyboards was made to fulfill the requirement of this assignment. The research report was attached with a reference list and an appendix. After that, it was submitted online.

Previous Next

WEEK 5

Not until I've submitted the paper that I've realized my mistake. This assignment was a visual research report, meanwhile I thought it was a typical research report. Hence my final mark was deducted. Furthermore, my tutor and co-ordinator was not very pleased with our project, they believed it was a rip-off of Uber Eats, Deliveroo or some similar apps that was popular in the mean time, and recommended us to change into other topic. After a long discussion within our team and some advices from our tutor, we decided to tag along with finding a type of autonomous vehicles to improve library experiences for both their customers and staffs.

Figure 1 - Apply observation and cognitive walkthrough on Anny

WEEK 6

Throughout this week, we were doing the whole research process again from the head start. Each member of our team would find ourselves at least 4 interviewees of both libary's customers and staffs to comprehend their concerns. Additionally, I have decided to create a prototype myself, in case we were short on time before we could actually finish this assignment with all needed components. The prototype was called "aDrone" and later changed its name into "Pigeon 1.0".

Figure 2 - "Pigeon 1.0" User Flow

WEEK 7

We held a group meeting this week to share all of our obtained data from last week. After that, we realized our mistake as we were too reliable on a autonomous drone system. Some librarian interviewees shown their concern that the usage of drone in a small area could cause harm to people. Hence, we seperated our ideations into two applications, one was "Pigeon" which helps students borrow books and the second is "iTrolley" which helps librarians manage the inside of the library. My initial idea was eliminated but it was still useful to help developing our final prototype and thus was included in our evaluation report as "Pigeon 1.0"

Figure 3 - Our initial ideas

WEEK 8

Our team decided to intergrate some extra methodologies to improve our research process that we initially did not do. Kingsy was doing a brainstorming to gather all the information we've gathered in the research process, while I was creating a context mapping to find a link between each groups and get a deeper understanding of what users know, feel and dream. Furthermore, we have made 3 low fidelity prototypes and started testing it with target group of users with the help of observation and cognitive walkthrough methodologies.

Figure 4 - Context Mapping

WEEK 9

Until this point, we've had all the needed components for this assignment. We shared our research reports and decided to choose "Pigeon 2.0" as the final ideation, since it has the best interface and easier to find target users. Kingsy began to make the mid-high fidelity prototype, meanwhile I was doing the evaluation and user flow for each low-fidelity one.

Figure 5 - Evaluation review for all low-fidelity prototypes

WEEK 10

Kingsy finished creating the mid-high fidelity prototype of "Pigeon 2.0" and started to evaluate his concept. In the meantime, I made the conclusion for our report and the next step for future assignment. After that, we reviewed our evaluation report to check for any encountered errors, then attached the reference list and appendix pages and submitted the whole report before it was too late. We devided the presentation slides between each members therafter.

Figure 6 - Mid-high fidelity "Pigeon 2.0" User Flow

WEEK 11

We took a short break after a huge group assignment then started to work on our process journal. Kingsy took the role of finishing the high-fidelity prototype and gave it to me and Brian to find a target group of people to test and record it.

Figure 7 - The first page of Pigeon website

WEEK 12

I made a walkthrough video for two high-fidelity prototypes and create a webpage for our project. Then I checked all the needed component for this website and deployed it to GitHub. Eventually, I compressed the file and submitted it.
